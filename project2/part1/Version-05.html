Hope
is the
thing
with
feathers
That
perches
in the
soul,
And sings
the
tune
without
the words,
And never
stops
at all,

And sweetest
in the
gale
is heard;
And sore
must be
the
storm
That
could
abash
the little
bird
That kept
so many
warm.

I've heard it
in the
chillest land,
And on the
strangest sea;
Yet, never,
in extremity,


It asked a crumb of me.
